One of the highlights of the US sporting calendar is the annual Super Bowl, the championship game of the National Football League. Las Vegas casinos typically see their highest sports betting revenues on the weekend of the game, and a large portion of this money comes from "prop bets" - individuals can wager whether certain events in the game will occur, and at what point in time. Prop bets, however, are not usually part of the every day Las Vegas sports book.

Betting with your friends at the game has always been a fun pastime. We propose taking things to the next level: pairing up fans at the stadium for user-specified prop bets, with payments sent anonymously via Bitcoin. Users will "check in" to the game they are attending, and can see a list of existing prop bets created by other users. If they wish to accept the challenge, they can wager Bitcoin on those bets, or create their own. Users will have the ability to set their own odds to make a given bet more or less attractive considering the payout. Betting will only be open to mobile users at the stadium, and new prop bets can only be made shortly before or during the event.

FAQ

Is this legal?
Laws regarding Internet-based gambling using Bitcoin are still in their infancy. If the government does not explicitly ban Bitcoin-based gaming, a lucrative opportunity exists if the site can go live quickly and gain market share.
What kind of privacy guarantees are there?
Payments are handled person-to-person, between two anonymous users. All communication between bettors is handled strictly through the server, and everything is encrypted to ensure maximum privacy.
